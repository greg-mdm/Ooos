Analysis: AI for XD

Research question: Can 3D immersion in demographic data shift perception from statistics to students?

Assignment: Mini-Project 1  ·  Course: Artificial Intelligence for Experiential Design

This experiment validates several design strategies for human-centred data visualization:

  • •1:1 scale in 3D brings individual visibility into view, while still enabling aggregate analysis.
  • •Interactive controls allow the viewer to reposition themselves in relation to the dataset.
  • •Immersive spatial navigation enhances individual recognition.
  • •Rotate-and-orbit controls visualize demographic information from a personal perspective.
  • •Zooming from different angles can reveal connections among individuals and groups in complex datasets.
Practical applications

Practical applications

  • •Exploring 3D data visualization fosters empathy and highlights the intersection of individual identities within demographic population groups. By applying design thinking principles to create immersive visualizations, we can adopt a “human-centered” approach with the potential to “drive innovation by deeply understanding” demographic data (IDEO U, 2026).
  • •In both 2D and 3D environments, methods for interpolating the path of individual instances have exciting potential applications. To map student mobility patterns, containers can be correlated with schools or study destinations, and the instanced mesh class can effectively capture study levels, nationality, gender, languages, and more.
